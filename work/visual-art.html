Visual Art

My artistic practice focuses on creating textured surfaces on canvas through the use of plaster, with a strong emphasis on color. I primarily work with cool tones, especially shades of blue, exploring the subtle interplay between light and shadow. To enhance these visual effects, I also incorporate interference acrylic paints, which create shifting reflections and luminous vibrations that change depending on the viewing angle and the surrounding light.

Plaster, adhesive, and acrylic on canvas, 25×30 cm, 2023–2026

Plaster, adhesive, and acrylic on canvas, 60×200 cm, 2025

My early paintings were characterized by cool, fluid colors applied in a way that resembled ink, allowing them to merge organically and generate new forms through a process of controlled spontaneity. This approach emphasized the energy and expressive gesture inherent in the act of painting. The initial inspiration came from aerial views of natural landscapes, particularly the morphology of rivers and the intricate patterns created by their branching networks.

Ink on mylar sheets, 30×30 cm, 2021

Inner Landscape is an exploration of the mapping of the imagination and the reconstruction of possible landscapes shaped by memory. The work is centered on the idea of place as something that takes form within our subconscious, revealing itself through an intimate image created by our inner world.

Rather than depicting a physical location, these landscapes emerge as mental and emotional spaces, where memory, perception, and imagination intertwine to create a deeply personal visual experience.

Cyanotype on paper, 30 × 20 cm, 2020
